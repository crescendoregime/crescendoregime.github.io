Crescendo Regime

The Crescendo Regime Manifesto

Authorized by Our Fearless Leader, the Supreme Conductor, Patriarch of the Pitch Police, Willemus Wheelington.


The Allegory of the Well

The allegory of the well is a central part of the Crescendo Regime's philosophy, as it outlines the perfect and harmonious structure of society as given to us by Our Fearless Leader.


The bucket in the well represents the Crescendo Regime as it is now - a constant, perfect harmony whose guiding principles will soon overcome our enemies through the valiant efforts of Our Fearless Leader.


The water that resides in the bucket represents the most loyal citizens of the Regime. Those who most closely follow the commands of the Regime will be lifted out of the darkness and into the light of the future.


However, the Scum of the Bucket that resides amidst the water is the political enemies of the Crescendo Regime, those who would defy order in service of chaos and disharmony. Once the bucket is lifted into the light and society has become perfect, the scum will cease to exist, a foul impurity cast aside by Our Fearless Leader's undying will.


The bricks in the well represent the proletariat, a constant force that underpins society's ascent. Though individually insignificant, the collective might of the proles allows the Crescendo Regime to thrive, and within them lies hope for the future of the Regime.


The Winch of the Bucket are the trusted advisors of Our Fearless Leader, and they serve to guide him in his noble purpose by conducting his orders and providing wise counsel on Regime matters. They guide the rope as it pulls the bucket out of the darkness and into the light.


Finally, the rope represents Our Fearless Leader himself, singlehandedly bringing about the ultimate future of the Crescendo Regime - one where the harmony of order and control resonates everywhere, and those who would defy the Crescendo Regime are silenced beneath its unity.




The Orchestra Pit

The ultimate punishment for those the Crescendo Regime deems as Scum of the Bucket is the Orchestra Pit, a lightless place whose walls are sunk into the earth such that overseers may look down upon prisoners. In this place, rebellion is silenced and obedience ensured.


Prisoners of the Pit partake in the cruelest of ironies - torture as an imitation of art. They polish rusted instruments until their fingers bleed, harvest reeds from mines deep within the earth, and copy entire opuses of sheet music by hand. Failure to comply is punished severely.


The most feared punishment of the Orchestra Pit is a place known as the Green Room, wherein those subjected to it must experience their deepest fears. This procedure serves to break the spirits of the Scum of the Bucket such that their minds fully accept the truth of the Crescendo Regime and the power of Our Fearless Leader.




The Scum of the Bucket

The Crescendo Regime names and recognizes its enemies as Scum of the Bucket in accordance with the teachings of Our Fearless Leader. Their impurity is held to the light and exposed for the heinous treachery that it is, and their names serve as a warning to all loyal citizens of the Regime and a constant reminder of the consequences of disobedience.


Emmanuel "Henry" Goldstein, now referred to as Mr. Illiterate, is the most reviled and notorious individual in the Crescendo Regime. Once a strong part of the Winch of the Bucket, Mr. Illiterate was exiled from the Regime for his dangerous notions of individuality and the belief that he could compose a melody outside of the Regime's tempo. His current title is given to demean him, as it serves to highlight the idiocy of his treachery against Our Fearless Leader.


Chairman Mannix, the Supreme Leader of Eastasia, is another enemy of the Crescendo Regime. His nation still follows the impure philosophies of Xi Jinping Thought whilst refusing to yield to the inexorable march of the Regime's world conquest. The Crescendo Regime has been at war with Eastasia since time immemorial, an eternal enemy that defies righteous erasure.


The nation of France also stands in military opposition to the Regime, clinging to the false revolutionary ideals of liberty, equality, and fraternity, originally set forth during the French Revolution. However, they are too stubborn to recognize that these delusions of freedom serve only to demolish the sacred truth that lies in the collective mind of the Regime. In time, they too will succumb to Our Fearless Leader's will.




Laudation of Exemplary Maestros

Two of the most distinguished figures in the Crescendo Regime are Yorak the Prophet, Grand Maestro of the Orchestra Pit, and Booster Gold, Maestro-in-Chief of the Pitch Police. They are the strongest parts of the Winch of the Bucket and the closest individuals to Our Fearless Leader within the Regime's hierarchy.


Yorak the Prophet's role in the Regime is to enlighten the bricks in the well and grant them noble purpose. His title of Prophet is not a religious rank, as the Crescendo Regime does not condone worship of deities. Rather, it exists as proof that Yorak is greater than the prophets of archaic faiths such as Christianity and Islam, as he preaches the mercy of Our Fearless Leader, a godlike figure in tangible form. In addition, Yorak oversees the Orchestra Pit and the Green Room to ensure that those who refuse enlightenment are properly reeducated, until their souls sing with joy for the Crescendo Regime's power.


Booster Gold acts as the overseer of the Pitch Police, an elite force whose purpose is to ensure perfect pitch and compliance among citizens. In this role, he wields tremendous power in ensuring that the Crescendo Regime remains free of impure thought, second only to Our Fearless Leader himself in this role. The Pitch Police observes every thought, word and action, and potential rebels are identified and captured to serve in the Orchestra Pit.




The Glorious Wounding

Our Fearless Leader has only been physically injured once in the entire recorded history of the Crescendo Regime. An assault from Eastasia, launched by the cowardly Chairman Mannix, came upon the shores of Port Hacking, a vital tactical location. In an unexpected yet unparalleled move, Our Fearless Leader was on the front lines, prepared to meet the attacking forces himself.


In the chaos of the battlefield, Our Fearless Leader's rifle was misplaced. Undeterred, he grabbed a canoe from the shoreline and used it as a weapon, knocking away tank shells left and right as he advanced towards the enemy.


One tank shell was not deflected by the canoe, but as it collided with Our Fearless Leader's left pinky, it shattered into fragments against his indestructible skin. Despite this, Our Fearless Leader dislocated the finger of his own volition out of pity for the foolish and shortsighted attack.


Soon after, a crocodile was deployed by Eastasia, and Our Fearless Leader quickly dispatched it with his bare hands. The fight aggravated his wound however, and Our Fearless Leader was swiftly escorted to Sutherland Hospital, the finest medical facility in the Regime.


At the hospital, Our Fearless Leader refused medical treatment, accepting only saccharine tablets and Victory Gin. His resilience is so great that this was all Our Fearless Leader required to have made a complete recovery by the end of that day.




The Octave Order and the Battle of Langley

The truth of the Crescendo Regime is that it is the final iteration of the Octave Order, an organisation whose influence spans all of recorded history. Humans have always understood the inherent power of music and the importance of ordered society, and Our Fearless Leader was the first to unite these concepts thousands of years ago.


The legendary Lancastrian king Henry IV was a powerful and respected member of the Order, and his righteous victory over the treacherous Henry Percy’s rebellion of 1667 would come to be known as the Battle of Jericho. Due to his exploits, modern-day Regime hoovers are proudly nicknamed Henrys in his honour. The hoover is both weapon and vehicle, the result of Our Fearless Leader's inventive genius. Its blasts of compressed air, evocative of a wind instrument, can flatten enemy soldiers and create a shield to deflect bullets. The suction mode of the hoovers is employed in public hooving executions performed on enemies of the state.


During the Battle of Langley, history repeated itself, as the Henrys warred against Henry just as Henry once fought Henry. The battle began on a cool evening in mid-January, the day after the Regime's annual journey to Broadway. During this journey, supplies were purchased and uniforms were donned in preparation for conflict. At once, the Crescendo Regime's Langley military stronghold was stormed by the forces of the cowardly Chairman Mannix and Henry Goldstein wielding powerful machine guns. Undaunted, Our Fearless Leader and his valiant soldiers manned the hoovers and charged into battle.


Long after the cowardly Chairman Mannix had escaped, the ever-villainous Goldstein made a pathetic last stand in the Menzies courtyard armed with a small machine gun. He then fled shortly after once he recognised the inevitable victory of the Regime's order over the disharmony of its enemies.


Once the Crescendo Regime secured its resounding triumph, Our Fearless Leader, through his otherworldly power, created the Elixir of Voice as a celebratory drink for himself and his trusted comrades-in-arms. Throughout the night, the soft sound of the ukulele could be heard throughout Langley. This sacred melody served as an unending testament to the glory of the Octave Order's grand purpose, fulfilled after millennia of struggle.
